Understanding Websites

A website is like a digital building made of interconnected pages, hosted on powerful computers called servers, and accessed through the internet. Just as you visit physical places, you "visit" websites using web addresses (URLs).

How Websites Work

When you visit a website, here's the amazing journey that happens in milliseconds:

1

You Type the Address

You enter a URL like "google.com" in your browser's address bar

2

Browser Finds the Server

Your browser asks the internet "Where can I find google.com?" and gets the server's location

3

Server Sends the Website

The web server sends back the website's files (HTML, CSS, images) to your browser

4

Browser Displays the Page

Your browser reads the files and displays the beautiful website you see

Why Understanding Websites Matters

Websites are the foundation of our digital world. They're how we:

  • Share information - From news to personal blogs
  • Connect with others - Social media and communication
  • Do business - Shopping, banking, and services
  • Learn and grow - Educational resources and tutorials
  • Express creativity - Art, music, and personal portfolios

By learning to build websites, you're gaining the power to shape the digital world!
